Dear Monica,

I read your wishlist. And here's the deal.
1. I can't write your thesis for you. Trust me, I tried. I am too dumb to decode hidden heritages. I don't even know what a phylogenetic analysis is if I'm being honest.
2. I tried finding some good zines. People just don't make good zines anymore. Where did all the good zines go? Well I did get you this Mountain Goats zine I found on reddit but I can't vouch for its quality.
3. I'm too incompetent to burn a CD. Also I have a Mac so, you know.

So instead I set out to make this interpretative hybrid of all three. This website will be a sort of

vaguely-your-thesis-inspired
annotated-mixtape-zine

At first I thought I should make a mixtape of songs that narrate folk tales, but then I realize I only know about four such songs (a devastating blow), so I broadened the concept a little. Since you study common ancestry in global folktales, as well as their evolution over time, the concept is "modern takes on folk music" and "cross-country folk music/modern music polination". As an avid folk listener, this is all also (an oddly specific corner of) the music I really like. Actually maybe this whole thing is just an excuse to make someone other than me in this world listen to False Lankum. Anyways, enjoy queen.
